The overflow of noise in our real world markets
catalyzes complex human behavior that injects disorder
and chaos to stock prices. The outcome? A random walk.
We all have seen it and we all know deep to our hearts
that in times of constant pressure prices are nothing
but a vapor wave.

If it's a gamble the least we can do is to make it fair.

Abstract markets are blind markets.

Which means: no real-time statistics, no price charts
and no chaos of bids and asks. There is two basic stages:
COMMITMENT and REVEAL. To explain it better let us introduce
our first market: Overpopulated.
At the very core of this economy and the concept of blind trust as a medium to guarantee efficiency is a very simple scheme of commit and reveal; a cryptographic primitive that if embedded to a smart contract mitigates the voluntary or involuntary manipulation of markets.

If all participants commit their actions without knowledge of the current state of an asset, the asset itself could be more stable. We will find out. Quantum Hall is about experimental economics, and Overpopulated is our first run.

In this enviroment you don't buy or sell stock, you migrate global citizens from country to country and there is no companies raising or falling in the charts, instead, a country is either stable or overpopulated.
COMMITMENT STAGE
The massive migration market will be open daily from 8:00 am to 5:00 pm EST.

During this stage, migration is self-regulated and owners can allocate
their citizens on any countries they wish. Each migration choice is submitted
to the network as a hashed secret and the new state of the globe will only be
known to the world after migration has concluded and all secrets revealed.

During open hours, the only information available to participants is the previous
global state and the counting number of qHalls migrating. The future state of each
an every country is unpredictable.
